Overview

Annie's Last Stand is a top-down shoot 'em up fused with real-time base defence, built solo in Unity in just under 3 weeks as a first-cycle university project (IGB100, QUT). Players take control of Annie, a lone elite operative defending the world's last nuclear silo against relentless waves of autonomous mechs. The mission: hold the courtyard long enough for four generators to complete the launch sequence.

Defeated enemies drop Scrap, which can be spent at an in-world shop on weapon upgrades, deployable turrets, and wall repairs — creating a continuous tension between immediate survival and long-term strategic investment. Enemies never stop coming, forcing constant repositioning, threat prioritisation, and multi-point defensive management.

The game's design had visual and tonal influences drawn from NieR: Automata, Honkai Impact, and Helldivers.

Technical Deep Dive

Combat & Projectile System

The player uses 360° free-aim combat with mouse tracking to control aiming direction. A bullet pooling manager handles the high volume of projectiles, avoiding runtime instantiation costs during intense firefights. Multiple weapon types were implemented with distinct fire rates, damage values, and spread patterns, each upgradeable via the scrap shop.

Enemy AI & Spawning

Rather than discrete wave rounds, enemies spawn continuously from drop ships that fly in at the map edges, maintaining constant pressure. Each of the seven enemy types has distinct targeting logic — ground units pathfind to generators, aerial units bypass walls entirely, and siege units prioritise structural damage. Drop ships themselves can be destroyed to temporarily slow the assault.

Scrap Economy & Shop System

Enemies drop Scrap on death, collected by proximity. An in-world shop near the generators lets players spend Scrap on turret deployment, wall placement, weapon upgrades, and generator repairs. This creates a meaningful risk-reward loop — pushing forward to farm Scrap versus retreating to spend it defensively.

Multi-Point Generator Defence

Four generators simultaneously contribute passive progress to the launch meter. Each can be independently damaged and destroyed — losing all four ends the mission. This multi-objective layout forces the player to constantly reposition and make strategic choices about where to invest their turrets and walls, as no single position can cover all four at once.

Enemy Roster

Seven enemy types plus a support unit create layered threat management across both ground and aerial dimensions.

Type Enemy Role
Ground Basic Mechs Backbone of every assault. Dangerous in numbers, fast to overwhelm unguarded positions.
Ground Laser Mechs Slower but deadly at range. Sustained laser fire chews through generators — always a priority target.
Ground Tanks Heavy armour, massive damage output. Walls barely slow them — bring maximum firepower.
Ground Transport Units Rush the perimeter and deploy troops on contact. Intercept before they arrive.
Aerial Basic Drones Fast and fragile. Fly over all ground defences and force constant repositioning.
Aerial Heavy Drones Tougher aerial units that absorb serious punishment before going down.
Aerial Gunships The most dangerous aerial threat. High durability, heavy firepower, targets generators directly.
Support Drop Ships Fly in at map edges and continuously offload troops. Destroying one buys critical breathing room.

Design Process

PX Goals & SHMUP Analysis

Three core player experience goals were defined: Urgency, Tension, and Strategic Mastery.

Key Design Revisions

The initial concept proposed a squad-based system with AI-controlled allies. Feedback identified that managing multiple agents would dilute the core SHMUP emphasis on precise movement and dodging. The design was revised to a single-player "lone operative" model, with strategic depth maintained through deployable turrets and walls instead.

A second revision replaced the original wave-based spawning system with continuous enemy pressure after feedback noted that downtime between waves reduced tension — working against the "Desperate Last Stand" experience goal.
